TOMORROWLAND
The biggest electronic music festival in the world

Introduction

In the mid-00s, the brothers Manu and Michiel Beers conceived the idea of â€‹creating this event, in a place near the municipality of Boom, Belgium.

The first edition of the festival took place on August 14, 2005.

The event offers settings and atmosphere that are surrounded by a decoration that simulates a world of magic and fantasy. The festival itself offers a variety of subgenres within electronic music.

Likewise, it consists of a campsite on the outskirts of the festival site, called DreamVille, for those attendees who wish to stay as close as possible to the festival.

Artists

Alan Walker is a British-born Norwegian DJ primarily known for the critically acclaimed single "Faded" (2015), which was certified platinum in 14 countries.

Martin Garrix is a Dutch DJ. His success began with the release of the song "Animals" on June 17, 2013. The song reached #1 in Belgium and the UK.

Steve Aoki is an American DJ of Japanese origin, founder of the record label Dim Mak Records. He is currently ranked #7 in the top 100 DJs in the world. His most played song is Waste It On Me.

Marshmello is an American producer and DJ of future bass, electronica and electrohouse. He started his career in 2015. His song (Alone) reached the Billboard Hot 100. He has collaborated with artists like Ookay, Jauz, and Slushii.

Curiosities

This event has more than 50 stages within the Tommorowland venue.

The inspiration for the Mainstage comes from the design they created in 2020 for the main stage of the virtual festival "Around the World".

In 2022, more than 700 DJs attended the festival with the aim of making all attendees enjoy.
